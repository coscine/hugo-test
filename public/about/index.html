About Coscine

Coscine is designed to integrate services for research data management into a single platform. It allows to manage multiple data sources such as gitlab or RDS object store for research data per project and offers enrichment with metadata support good scientific pratices. Furthermore it integrates a simple access management to include external partners into your research projects.

The concepts behind Coscine.

Coscine aims to provide support for research data management along the research data lifecycle. The goal is to integrate and enrich established services and workflows with metadata management rather than re-inventing existing services with minimal changes. We think that collaborative work on data is key to scientific success. Therefore, easy access for collegues and external partners is an integral part of Coscine.

What is an Integration Environment?

Our approach is to integrate and enrich established services and workflows.

We do not want to break or re-invent processes but try to provide additional value by connecting otherwise separate services. To achieve this, we leverage contextual information provided by local identidy management services, publicly available organizational information and stadardized interfaces for access management.

Ultimately we strive to enable researchers to not only fullfill the requirements of good scientific practice but to enable natively digital research processes.

Integration of Multiple Resources

Resources a are key conept in Coscine. We look at data sources and services like object store, Gitlab or cloud provider as resources that are integrated into a research project.

FAIR Principles

The FAIR principles are a guide to make research data open and available while allowing the researcher to maintaining ownership over his research. Coscine follows those principles and enables simple data storage solutions to become FAIR.

Application Profiles for Your Needs

Applications profiles are key to describe research data with metadata. We aim to solve the common trade-off between specificity and generalization of metadata by allowing to use multiple application profiles for each project. So you can select the best schema to describe your data on a fine grained level while keeping everything in accordance with established standards.
